    Mutt Love Rescue USDA Stock Up

Overview

Stock Up is a mobile application that allows users to create, customize, compare, and interact with their grocery shopping list according to different store locations. Shoppers benefit from an itemized view of the products they need compared to the total cost of their list at a different store location because it helps optimize and expedite their decision making.

User Research

We conducted 5 user interviews with participants of varying budget sizes for grocery expenses. We formulated questions to learn about the behaviors and mindsets of shoppers during the COVID-19 pandemic, as well as during normal circumstances. We sorted our interview data into an affinity diagram and empathy map to inform our user persona.

Given the responses gathered from the user interviews, it was clear that shoppers had a problem gaining quick access to adequate substitutes when their first choice items were unavailable. The direct competitor in the App Store that most closely resembled Stock Up was Basket. Even though it enabled price comparison, filtering, and shopping list interactivity, it forced the user to select brands before they could see price. It also did not allow the user to add repeat items to a favorites list. Users also commented on a bug where suggested items would replace their selected items and mess up their shopping list.

Problem Statement

It was observed that grocery shoppers select stores where they can stay within their budget and minimize travel time. During the COVID-19 pandemic, staple grocery items that are both affordable and good quality quickly go out of stock, which adds to the time spent wandering the store aisles searching for substitutes or blindly visiting multiple stores.

Iterations

Decreasing the number of actions on the Home screen to not overwhelm a new user.
On the “Choose Your Basket” screen, we grouped similarly priced stores together to help the user spot more subtle price differences for their items.
The “Out of Stock” button was changed to read “Sold Out” because we would not send a user to a store where their items are out of stock.

Reflection

Completing a cognitive walk-through with the competitor apps would have been useful during the research phase to identify more opportunities and gaps in their user flow. We should have spent more time on creating more of a story arc in our case study by building out the resolution more in a journey map and later in our prototype.
